# AB-PMJAY / HBP Healthcare Cost Guide

## What is AB-PMJAY?
India's government health insurance scheme (Ayushman Bharat) covering hospitalization costs. Rates come from the Health Benefit Package (HBP) 2022, published by the National Health Authority.

## What is covered?
- Inpatient hospitalization across 26 medical/surgical specialties
- 919 packages covering 1,670 procedures
- Pre-procedure investigations (blood tests, imaging) — bundled in package
- 15 days post-discharge medicines — bundled in package
- Follow-up consultation — bundled in package
Each HBP package is a bundled all-in rate: admission to discharge to 15-day follow-up.

## What is NOT covered (always OPD/excluded)?
- Outpatient (OPD) consultations and treatment only
- Cosmetic / aesthetic surgery
- Dental procedures (unless trauma/accident)
- Vaccination and immunization
- Infertility and IVF treatments
- Routine health check-ups
- Drug de-addiction programs

## City Tier Classification (HBP X/Y/Z)
Procedure rates differ by city tier:
- **Tier 1 (X) — highest rates**: Delhi UA, Mumbai UA, Kolkata UA, Chennai UA, Bengaluru UA, Ahmedabad UA, Hyderabad UA, Pune UA
- **Tier 2 (Y) — mid rates**: State capitals and large cities — Jaipur, Nagpur, Lucknow, Surat, Kanpur, Bhopal, Patna, Chandigarh, Coimbatore, etc.
- **Tier 3 (Z) — base rates**: All other cities, towns, and rural areas

## Cost Components (4 parts)
1. **Procedure Base** — HBP 2022 procedure rate for the patient's city tier
2. **Hospital Stay** — Bed-day rate × estimated length of stay:
   - Routine Ward: ₹2,300/day
   - High Dependency Unit (HDU): ₹3,630/day
   - ICU (no ventilator): ₹9,350/day
   - ICU (with ventilator): ₹9,900/day
3. **Medications** — 8–12% of procedure base (IRDAI benchmark for 15-day post-discharge meds)
4. **Contingency** — 9% of subtotal (unexpected tests, minor complications)

## Multi-Procedure Stacking Rule (HBP Section 1.3)
When multiple procedures are done in one operation session:
- Primary procedure (highest cost): billed at 100%
- Second procedure: billed at 50%
- Third and beyond: billed at 25% each
This is why some steps show "×50% stacked" or "×25% stacked".

## What does "Bundled" mean on a pathway step?
Steps showing "Bundled" (typically consultations, diagnostics, follow-ups) are included within the main procedure package rate. They do not add separately to the total cost.

## Confidence Score
- **≥75%**: High confidence — symptoms map clearly to condition, estimate is reliable
- **60–74%**: Moderate confidence — directionally correct, verify with a doctor
- **<60%**: Low confidence — needs clarification for accurate estimate

## Government vs Private Hospital Costs
The base estimate reflects government/HBP rates (1.0× multiplier).
- Mid-tier private hospitals: typically 1.5–2.0× the base estimate
- Corporate / super-specialty hospitals: typically 2.5–3.5× the base estimate
The Matched Hospitals tab shows adjusted estimates per hospital type.

## Why costs can be higher in practice
- NABH-accredited hospitals add 10–15% to package rates (quality incentive)
- Metro-located hospitals add another 10%
- ICU requirement (if condition worsens) significantly increases stay cost
- Pre-existing conditions (diabetes, hypertension) may extend length of stay
- Implants (joint replacements, stents, pacemakers) are billed separately

## Disclaimer
All estimates are for pre-decision planning only. Actual costs vary by hospital, patient condition, and case complexity. This is not medical advice. Consult a qualified physician before any healthcare decision.
